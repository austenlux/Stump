# Rules

## I - Materials Required

* At least 2 players
* 1 stump
* 1 hammer
* 1 nail for each player. These nails must all be the same type

## II - Setting Up

### II.A - Nail Placement

Begin by hammering 1 nail for each player into the stump. All nails should be placed along the same ring of the stump and at least 2 inches away from any other nails if possible. Otherwise, they should be placed in whatever area maximizes distance from nearby nails.
Nails may not be placed into cracks or holes in the wood such that hammering that nail is significantly easier than other nails. Exceptions to this may be made if explicitly agreed upon by players as a method of handicap.
All nails should be hammered to an equal, agreed upon depth.
All players must stand around the stump by their nail.

### II.B - Establishing Turn Order

If this is the first game of Stump, players choose and agree upon a way to decide who goes first.
If this is not the first game of Stump, the player who won the last game gets the first turn.
Turns will proceed clockwise from that player.

## III - Overview

Each player has their own nail placed in the stump. On your turn, you flip the hammer, catch it, and strike another player's nail. The goal is to be the last player whose nail has not been completely driven into the stump.

A player is eliminated when their nail has been completely hammered into the stump. The last player remaining wins.

## IV - Taking a Turn

A turn consists of 3 phases:

* Flip phase
* Strike phase
* Drink phase

### IV.A - Flip

During the Flip phase, a player throws the hammer into the air so that it flips vertically (head over handle). The player then attempts to catch the hammer after it flips at least once in the air.

The number of vertical flips is the number of strikes the player may make in the Strike phase.

The player may not adjust their grip on the hammer. The way the player catches the hammer is how they must perform their strikes. If they adjust the grip on their hammer or fail to catch the hammer, they must drink and immediately end their turn, passing the hammer to the next player in turn order.

Upon catching the hammer, the player must immediately move to the Strike phase without hesitation.

### IV.B - Strike

Upon catching the hammer, the player must immediately perform a strike. The player may not hesitate or aim, though they are allowed to bring the hammer up and down again after catching.

The player must strike another player's nail. A player may not strike their own nail intentionally. If a player has earned multiple strikes, they may split them across different players' nails.

If a player has previously bent another player's nail, they are locked onto that nail. They must continue striking that bent nail until it is either straightened or fully driven into the stump. They may not strike any other player's nail while locked.

A miss is not penalized.

A nail is considered finished if all players agree it is completely hammered into the stump or if the Hammer Test decides it is (see [Hammer Test](#v---hammer-test)). Any player may challenge a nail and perform a Hammer Test. This effectively pauses the game. Giving away the hammer for someone to perform the Hammer Test does not end your turn, and that player must return the hammer to you upon completing the Hammer Test, resuming the game.

### IV.C - Drink

When a player's nail is struck, that player must drink. A "Drink" is a consistent unit which must be decided upon before the game begins.

The player then passes the hammer to the next player in turn order.

## V - Hammer Test

A Hammer Test can be performed by any player to determine if a nail has been completely hammered into the stump. The hammer is turned sideways and the head is run over the nail from all angles. The nail is considered completely hammered in if the hammer does not clink with the nail. If everyone agrees a nail is completely hammered in then the Hammer Test can be skipped. Any player may challenge another player's nail during their turn via the Hammer Test.

If the Hammer Test is performed during another player's turn and that player gives up the hammer, it does not count as forfeiting the hammer. The hammer must be returned to that player after the test is complete.

## VI - Bent Nails

If a player bends another player's nail during a strike, that player becomes locked onto the bent nail. They must continue striking that nail on subsequent turns until it is either straightened or fully driven into the stump. While locked, the player may not strike any other player's nail.

## VII - Penalties

If a player strikes a different player's nail while locked onto a bent nail, that player must drink 1.

## VIII - Sparks

If a spark is observed when the hammer strikes a nail, any player may call "Spark!" and all players must take a drink.
